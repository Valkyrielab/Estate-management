CONFIDENTIALITY and the PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION ACT, 2013 (“POPIA”)

Client confidentiality is of paramount importance in the providing of financial services to our clients. As required in terms of POPIA, all Personal Information (“PI”) disclosed by the Client to Legacy remains confidential unless the Client provides written consent to disclose such information or Legacy is required by law or it is in the public interest to disclose such information. In disclosing such information, Legacy is required to consider the rights of the parties concerned.

Please take note of the following:

  1. A Client’s PI will be used by Legacy to enable it to provide suitable financial services to the Client as required in terms of FAIS.
  2. If the Client is acting on behalf of another person, then the Client warrants that he/she has the required authority from the Client to act on behalf of such Client and to provide Legacy with the Client’s PI.
  3. The Client’s PI will be disclosed or transferred to third parties with the aim to provide suitable financial services to the Client and/or to give effect to the financial transaction entered into by the Client.

These Parties may include the following:

  1. Product Suppliers as indicated above or disclosed in documentation and application forms to the Client;
  2. Services Providers to whom material aspects of services are outsourced to enhance financial services support to Clients;
  3. Essential Services Providers who may have access to the Client’s PI as required in terms of legislation to oversee compliance and enhance protection of PI in the business of Legacy, and may include (amongst others):
    • Compliance: Compli-Serve (SA) (Pty) Ltd and associated companies – as required in terms of FAIS;
    • Regulators: Financial Sector Conduct Authority (“FSCA”), Information Regulator (“IR”), Financial Intelligence Centre (“FIC”) etc.
    • Accounting Officers/Auditors:as required in terms of FAIS;
    • Information Technology Service Providers:to ensure safekeeping and protection of PI;
  1. Any other third parties not indicated above who may have access to the Client’s PI, will be disclosed to the Client upfront and is subject to consent by the Client.
  2. The Client gives specific consent for its PI to be used for the following purposes (tick applicable):
    1. Newsletters to the Client’s email address
    2. Referral for wills and estate planning
  3. The Client warrants that the PI provided by him/her is true, correct and complete and undertakes to inform Legacy timeously of any changes to enable Legacy to keep it updated.
  4. Legacy has a PAIA and POPIA Manual in place setting out what how it deals with the Clients’ PI, how to update its PI or to delete its PI. Legacy is not allowed to disclose a Clients PI and should a party having an interest in such PI wish to obtain access to the Client’s PI, then Legacy’s PAIA and POPIA Manual’, setting out the process to be followed is available on Legacy’s website www.legacyfs.co.za or e-mailing the Information Officer at smukadam@legacyfs.co.za or Deputy Information Officer at ibloom@legacyfs.co.za
  5. Unauthorised disclosure of PI may result in a breach by Legacy which may be reportable to the Information Regulator (“IR”) in a manner and form prescribed by the IR. Details on the reporting process can be obtained from www.justice.gov.za and the IR contact details are: Email: complaints.IR@justice.gov.za.
